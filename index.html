About Project Clarity

The Clarity for Humans project is a longitudinal observation framework designed to catalog human moral and emotional decision-making over a continuous 100-year span. By presenting a daily singular choice, the sequence eliminates variable noise and focuses on the core cognitive response of the participant. The project operates on a fixed temporal anchor, ensuring that all data points are mapped to a specific progression of social and existential inquiry.

Data harvested through this framework is intended for retrospective analysis by future sociological entities. The clinical isolation of the choice environment prevents external influence, promoting an unbiased choice recording process. Participants are viewed as vital components of a long-term data pool, contributing to the collective understanding of human value evolution over the 21st century.

Maintenance of the sequence is strictly static. The architecture is designed for zero-intervention operation, relying on the participant's internal consistency and the reliability of local cryptographic storage. The study concludes on December 31st, 2125.

Privacy and Data Governance

This study adheres to a zero-transmission policy. No personal identifiers, decision data, or telemetry is transmitted to external servers or cloud repositories. All records of choice are held exclusively in the participant's LocalStorage environment on the primary access device. The CFH Research Foundation has no capacity to view, modify, or retrieve individual results.
